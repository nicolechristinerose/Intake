07 — Informed Consent

Scope of Practice. Massage therapy is provided for the purposes of relaxation, stress reduction, relief of muscular tension, and general wellness. Massage therapists do not diagnose medical conditions, prescribe treatments, or practice medicine. The information shared on this form is used solely to tailor your session safely.

Voluntary Participation. You have the right to end your session or refuse any technique at any time, for any reason, without explanation. Your comfort and safety are the highest priority.

Informed Disclosure. You are encouraged to communicate openly about pressure, positioning, pain, or discomfort during your session. It is your responsibility to disclose all relevant health information, including conditions, medications, and recent procedures.

Possible Effects. Some soreness, fatigue, or emotional release may occur after a session, particularly with deeper work. These are generally transient. Please hydrate well and rest as needed.

Privacy. All information shared on this form is kept confidential and will not be shared without your written consent, except as required by law.

Minors. Clients under 18 years of age must have written consent from a parent or legal guardian, who must remain on-site during the session.
